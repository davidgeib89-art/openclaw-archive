In condensed matter physics, Floquet time crystals are periodically driven many-body systems that respond at subharmonic frequencies of the driving period \(Else, Bauer and Nayak, 2016\). The stability of these systems depends on maintaining a balance between the driving input and suppression of rapid thermalization. The essential requirements are a source of periodic modulation, an intrinsic oscillator network capable of coupling to the drive, and a mechanism for maintaining fixed phase relationships across oscillatory modes. Microtubules possess the latter two features by virtue of their polyatomic oscillator hierarchy, which maintains stable phase relationships from

But there are biologically plausible periodic drives for Floquet time crystal behaviour in microtubules. 

Mitochondrial ultraweak photon emission \(UPE\) offers a credible candidate for photonic input to neuronal microtubules. ## **8. Conclusion **

In condensed matter physics, a crystal is a solid whose constituents are arranged in a spatially periodic lattice, breaking continuous spatial-translational symmetry. A ‘ *time crystal*’ is a phase of matter in which the state of a system exhibits periodic dynamical structure in time, breaking continuous *time * translational symmetry. Time crystals are nested hierarchies, ‘clocks within clocks within clocks…’, patterns of oscillations repeating at multiple frequencies including a lowest energy quantum ground state. 

Time crystals were proposed as theoretical possibilities by Nobel laureate Frank Wilczek \(2012\), after which they began to be implemented using various quantum technologies at ultra-low temperatures.
